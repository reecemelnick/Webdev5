Explore Vancouver Island!

Get wild!

Vancouver Island, located off the southwestern coast of British Columbia, Canada, is a captivating destination renowned for its diverse natural beauty and vibrant communities. Stretching over 460 kilometers from north to south, the island offers a wide range of landscapes, from rugged coastlines and temperate rainforests to towering mountains and serene lakes. Visitors can explore the picturesque capital city of Victoria with its charming heritage architecture, or venture into the wilder parts of the island for outdoor adventures like hiking, whale watching, and surfing. Vancouver Island's cultural scene is equally enticing, featuring a rich indigenous heritage, artistic communities, and a thriving food and wine culture. With its mild climate, friendly locals, and an abundance of outdoor and cultural experiences, Vancouver Island is a true gem on Canada's west coast, attracting travelers from around the world.

Heart Lake, Ladysmith

Heart Lake is a picturesque natural gem nestled in the stunning landscapes of British Columbia, Canada. Surrounded by lush forests and majestic mountains, this pristine alpine lake offers a serene and tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. The crystal-clear waters of Heart Lake are perfect for swimming, kayaking, and fishing, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.

Wild Pacific Trail, Ucluelet

The Wild Pacific Trail is located in Ucluelet and is a seven phase trail system skirting the rugged cliffs and shoreline of the west coast of Vancouver Island. Overlooking Barkley Sound and the Broken Group Islands to the east and the open Pacific Ocean to the south and west, it offers spectacular shoreline panoramas and seaward vistas through ancient cedar and spruce-framed viewing platforms constructed on the best headlands along the route.

Juan De Fuca Marine Trail, Sooke

juan de Fuca Park on the west coast of southern Vancouver Island offers scenic beauty, spectacular hiking, marine and wildlife viewing, and roaring surf in its course along the Pacific coastline of the Strait of Juan de Fuca. There are four main areas to the park: the China Beach Campground, the Juan de Fuca Marine Trail, the China Beach day-use area, and Botanical Beach.

Rain Forrest Trail, Ucluelet

The rainforest trail in Ucluelet is a breathtaking natural wonder nestled on the west coast of Vancouver Island, Canada. This enchanting trail winds through a lush and vibrant temperate rainforest, offering a mesmerizing journey through towering cedar and fir trees, moss-covered rocks, and the soothing sounds of cascading waterfalls and babbling streams. The rich biodiversity of the rainforest trail provides a home to an array of wildlife.
